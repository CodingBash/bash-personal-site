Hello!



My name is Basheer Becerra (pronounced as BUH-sheer bi-SE-ruh)!

I am currently an undergraduate senior attending Illinois State University and double majoring in computer science and statistics with a biology minor.

For my whole life, I've lived in a rural town of El Paso, IL. Originally focusing on athletics, specifically soccer, I've left the sport during the end of high school to focus more on academics and personal growth. I originally started studying computer science near the end of high school. By discovering a field and hobby that I loved to spend time on, I quickly developed my computer science skills. After being accepted to State Farm's IT internship the summer after high school graduation, computer science became a field that I wanted to contribute to throughout my career. Many college students discover another passion during the beginning of college - for me, that was life sciences. I soon integrated these two interests to spark a newfound interest in computational biology. After a bit of experience, I knew that this was the primary field I wanted to pursue. Even though my university didn't provide a formal program for computational biology, I independently forged my own path by working with biology and computer science professors, independently learning relevant material, taking as many courses in each of the sub-disciplines of computational biology, and pursuing research opportunities and internships. My ambition was to best prepare myself for graduate school research, although my desire was to explore a field that I loved studying. Fast forward to now, I currently possess 3.5 years of industry internship experience, participated in multiple academic research projects, double majored in computer science and statistics with a minor in biology, and developed myself professionally to take the next step in my education!

Bioinformatics Undergraduate Researcher - January 2017 – Present

Principal Investigator: Dr. Alysia Vrailas-Mortimer
Project Title: “Proteomic Analysis of p38 in Drosophila melanogaster.”
I independently performed proteomic analysis of differentially expressed proteins in short-lived p38K knockout, long-lived p38K over-expression, and wild-type Drosophila melanogaster brains and muscles to discover mechanisms in aging and age-dependent diseases. Project Title: “FlyCAGE: Correlational Analysis of Gene Expression in Drosophila melanogaster.”
I independently developed a bioinformatics web tool that conducts analysis of Drosophila melanogaster (fruit fly) mRNA expression data to discover genes with correlated spatiotemporal expression.

Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory
Quantitative Biology Undergraduate Research Program Participant - June 2018 – August 2018

Principal Investigator: Dr. Alexander Krasnitz
Project Title: “Copy Number Analysis (CNA) of Pancreatic Cancer Organoids to Predict Drug Response.”
I performed genomic profiling of organoids derived from patients with pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC). I developed a method using statistical learning and other pre-processing algorithms to predict chemotherapeutic drug response using extracted copy-number features. This analysis was also used to characterize molecular subtypes of PDAC based on their extracted copy-number features.
